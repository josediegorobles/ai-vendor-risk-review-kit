## Who It Is For

Small and mid-sized companies that are evaluating, buying, or expanding an AI tool and need a technical procurement memo before rollout.

Typical triggers:

- A department wants to start using ChatGPT, Copilot, Notion AI, or a vertical AI SaaS.
- The company will upload customer, employee, legal, financial, health, or operational data.
- Procurement needs better vendor questions before signing.
- Leadership wants an AI Act and governance sanity check without commissioning a full compliance project.
- A law firm or consultant needs technical input to support their own advisory work.

## Deliverable

The output is a concise Markdown/PDF-ready memo covering:

- Executive summary
- Company adoption scenario
- Data exposure map
- AI Act role hypothesis with caveats
- Security and privacy procurement questions
- Operational dependency and lock-in risks
- Human oversight checklist
- Recommended controls
- Final recommendation: Go, Go with controls, or Pause
- Evidence appendix with public sources

## Inputs Needed

- Tool/vendor under review
- Intended use case
- User groups
- Data categories expected to enter the tool
- Whether outputs affect people, customers, hiring, credit, health, safety, or legal decisions
- Existing security constraints: SSO, DLP, retention, logging, DPA, data residency, vendor approval process
- Any vendor links, contracts, or security docs available to the company

## Boundaries

This is a technical procurement review. It is not legal advice, a certification, a pentest, a DPIA, a full AI Act assessment, or a compliance guarantee.
